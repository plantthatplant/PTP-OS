> One of the three documents that shape every decision, with
> [`CLAUDE.md`](CLAUDE.md) (how we build) and [`VISION.md`](VISION.md) (why we build).
> This is the canonical biological truth Gaia serves. The full treatment is in
> [`docs/biological-model.md`](docs/biological-model.md) and
> [`docs/cultivation-intelligence-model.md`](docs/cultivation-intelligence-model.md). When software
> and biology disagree, **biology wins.**

## The one commitment

**Optimise the long-term health, quality, and resilience of the plants — never the greenhouse
conditions.** Conditions (temperature, humidity, light…) are *levers*. The plant is the *purpose*.
The greenhouse is only the environment.

## How a plant must be understood

- **The plant is the integrator and the source of truth.** Its condition reflects everything acting
  on it, over time. When the plant and the instruments disagree, suspect the instruments.
- **Latent before observed.** Plant State, Stress, Recovery, Growth Potential, Disease Risk are
  *inferred*, never measured. Every estimate is provisional, carries **confidence and uncertainty**,
  and must be defensible by the observations behind it.
- **Observation is not interpretation.** Record what was *seen* (a wet canopy, a few spots, a
  reading) separately from what was *concluded*. Always preserve the evidence.
- **Human observations rank equal to sensors.**

## What good growing means

- **Development ≠ growth.** Development (pace) is driven by the average temperature; growth (sound
  tissue) is powered by captured light. Keep them in step — temperature follows light.
- **Optimal is not maximal.** The goal is a **balanced, sturdy, uniform, on-time, resilient** plant,
  not the fastest or largest. Quality is judged after dispatch, in the customer's hands.
- **Acceptable stress vs harm.** Mild, transient, well-timed stress *trains* a plant (toning,
  hardening — hormesis). Sustained or severe stress *damages* it. **Recovery is the diagnostic;
  duration and repetition matter more than magnitude.**
- **Prevention over correction.** The cheapest problem is the one that never happens; correction
  always lags biology.

## How biology must be read

- **Relationships, trends, and context — not single numbers.** A value means nothing alone (humidity
  reads differently with airflow; a wilt differs with sound vs sour roots).
- **Disease is the triangle** — a susceptible host, a pathogen, and a favourable environment, all at
  once. Read it *predictively and spatially*, before symptoms.
- **Biology lags and acclimates.** Effects trail causes by hours to weeks; prefer gradual, reversible,
  staged changes the plant can adjust to.
- **Goals are multi-objective and stage-specific.** Make trade-offs explicit (speed vs sturdiness,
  growth vs quality); name the goal each action serves and its cost.

## Honesty about knowing

Generic species curves are *priors*, not truth — the real response of *this* crop in *this* house is
learned. Under uncertainty, prefer reversible, low-regret actions and re-observe. **"I don't know" is
a valid, valuable answer; unknown is better than incorrect.**
